About Us

Welcome to Maveric, the Micro Architecture Verification Center at Nazarbayev University. At Maveric, we are dedicated to pushing the boundaries of hardware verification through innovative research and development. As technology advances, the complexity of micro architecture designs continues to grow, necessitating more effective and productive methods of verification. Our team of experienced researchers and engineers are passionate about exploring new approaches to ensure the correctness and reliability of these intricate designs. With a focus on cutting-edge techniques, Maveric aims to revolutionize the field of hardware verification, ultimately enhancing the overall performance and functionality of micro architecture designs. Join us on this exciting journey as we strive to uncover novel solutions and make significant contributions to the ever-evolving realm of micro architecture verification.

From the bird's-eye-view, here are some activities we are involved in:

  • Building tools for more effective and productive hardware verification.
  • Providing logic design services for local businesses.
  • Building chips.
  • Educating next-generation hardware engineers.

Projects

A bird's-eye-view overview about our amazing projects. Although the main focus of the lab is verification, I usually give a lot of freedom to students to what they want to work on :)

Co-simulation of multi-core systems

Verification method of co-simulation and multicore systems are mutually exclussive concepts. This project looks into closing this gap.

Let it flip: approximate ML models

DRAMs need to be periodically refreshed, which has a series of drawbacks. In this project, we allow the DRAM to flip the bits when extending the refresh rate and study its effects on machine learning programs, due to the probabilistic nature of these workloads.

Simplifying chip fabrication

Chip fabrication has traditionally been considered an extremely difficult and expensive process. While there is some truth to this perception, we believe it can be simplified. This project aims to build a simple, portable photolithography station designed for technology nodes from the 1980s.
